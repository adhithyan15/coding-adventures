1 defmodule CodingAdventures.Repl.Language do
2 @moduledoc """
3 The Language behaviour — the heart of the REPL's pluggability.
4
5 ## What Is a Language?
6
7 In a REPL, a *language* is anything that can take a string of text and
8 produce a result. That result is one of three things:
9
10 1. `{:ok, String.t()}` — evaluation succeeded and there is a value to show.
11 2. `{:ok, nil}` — evaluation succeeded but there is nothing to display
12 (e.g. an assignment that produces no value, like `x = 5` in Python).
13 3. `{:error, String.t()}` — evaluation failed with a message to display.
14 4. `:quit` — the user wants to exit the session.
15
16 ## Why a Behaviour?
17
18 Using a behaviour (Elixir's equivalent of a protocol or interface) lets the
19 REPL loop remain completely agnostic about the language it is evaluating.
20 You can plug in:
21
22 - A trivial echo language (for testing and demos)
23 - A mathematical expression evaluator
24 - A full Elixir interpreter
25 - A Brainfuck engine
26 - Anything else that can map `String.t() → result`
27
28 The loop only knows about this contract. Nothing more.
29
30 ## Implementing a Language
31
32 ```elixir
33 defmodule MyMathLanguage do
34 @behaviour CodingAdventures.Repl.Language
35
36 @impl true
37 def eval(":quit"), do: :quit
38 def eval(input) do
39 case Integer.parse(input) do
40 {n, ""} -> {:ok, Integer.to_string(n * 2)}
41 _ -> {:error, "not a number: \#{input}"}
42 end
43 end
44 end
45 ```
46 """
47
