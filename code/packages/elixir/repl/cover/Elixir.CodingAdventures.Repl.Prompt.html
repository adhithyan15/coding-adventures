2 @moduledoc """
3 The Prompt behaviour — controls what the REPL displays to the user.
4
5 ## Two Kinds of Prompt
6
7 Classic interactive shells distinguish between two prompts:
8
9 1. **Global prompt** — shown at the start of a new expression. This is the
10 familiar `> ` or `iex> ` that you see when the REPL is waiting for fresh
11 input. It signals "I am ready; give me something new."
12
13 2. **Line prompt** — shown on continuation lines when an expression spans
14 multiple input lines. In Python you see `... ` when you open a block;
15 in iex you see `...> `. It signals "I got your first line; keep going."
16
17 ## Why Two Callbacks?
18
19 Because the loop needs to know which one to show without reaching into the
20 prompt module's internals. The caller decides based on context — for now
21 the framework always shows the global prompt (single-line input model), but
22 multi-line language implementations can use line_prompt themselves when they
23 detect an incomplete expression.
24
25 ## Implementing a Prompt
26
27 ```elixir
28 defmodule MyPrompt do
29 @behaviour CodingAdventures.Repl.Prompt
30
31 @impl true
32 def global_prompt(), do: "calc> "
33
34 @impl true
35 def line_prompt(), do: " ... "
36 end
37 ```
38 """
