3 PipelineToken -- a unit of work flowing through the pipeline.
4
5 Think of it as a tray on an assembly line. The tray starts empty at the
6 IF stage, gets filled with decoded information at ID, gets computed
7 results at EX, gets memory data at MEM, and delivers results at WB.
8
9 The token is ISA-independent. The ISA decoder fills in the fields via
10 callbacks. The pipeline itself never looks at instruction semantics --
11 it only moves tokens between stages and handles stalls/flushes.
12
13 ## Token Lifecycle
14
15 IF stage: FetchFunc fills in PC and raw_instruction
16 ID stage: DecodeFunc fills in opcode, registers, control signals
17 EX stage: ExecuteFunc fills in alu_result, branch_taken, branch_target
18 MEM stage: MemoryFunc fills in mem_data (for loads)
19 WB stage: WritebackFunc uses write_data to update register file
20
21 ## Bubbles
22
23 A "bubble" is a special token that represents NO instruction. Bubbles
24 are inserted when the pipeline stalls (to fill the gap left by frozen
25 stages) or when the pipeline flushes (to replace discarded speculative
26 instructions). A bubble flows through the pipeline like a normal token
27 but does nothing at each stage.
28
29 In hardware, a bubble is a NOP (no-operation) instruction. In our
30 simulator, it is a token with `is_bubble: true`.
31 """
