3 Backend-neutral draw scene primitives.
4
5 This module separates producer logic from renderer logic:
6
7 - **Producer packages** decide *what* should be drawn (rectangles, text,
8 lines, groups, clip regions).
9 - **Renderer packages** decide *how* to serialize or paint that scene
10 (SVG, PNG, Canvas, terminal, etc.).
11
12 ## Scene model
13
14 A scene is a flat list of instructions wrapped in a `DrawScene` struct
15 that carries overall dimensions and a background colour. Instructions
16 are plain maps tagged with a `:kind` atom:
17
18 | Kind | Purpose |
19 |---------|-------------------------------------------|
20 | `:rect` | Filled or stroked rectangle |
21 | `:text` | Positioned text label |
22 | `:line` | Straight line segment between two points |
23 | `:group`| Semantic grouping of child instructions |
24 | `:clip` | Rectangular clip region for children |
25
26 ## Metadata
27
28 Every instruction carries an optional `metadata` map. Metadata lets
29 producers attach domain meaning (e.g. a barcode character index)
30 without polluting the shared scene model.
31
32 ## Renderer behaviour
33
34 Any module that implements `render/1` can serve as a renderer.
35 The convenience function `render_with/2` also accepts a map with
36 a `:render` key holding a 1-arity function, which is handy for
37 one-off or test renderers.
38 """
